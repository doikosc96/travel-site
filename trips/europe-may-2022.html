Our first adventure together

It was a planned trip — exciting but a little anxious. We had never been out of the country together before, so we didn't really know how each other traveled. We started dating in January 2020, and while we had moved in together by this point it was still a relatively new relationship. I was 24, Madeleine was 22. Young, which made it all the more exciting.

Very romantic, very adventurous, but slow. We both found pretty quickly that we like to relax in a city — vacation as if we live there. A couple of big things we want to hit over a few days but no rush, just flowing through with an ease and lightness. Flitting in and out of shops, stopping at a café for a snack or an espresso, the occasional guilty cigarette here and there.

Paris

France

Paris is the city of love. Waking up early while Madeleine slept, reading Camus at a café, then bringing back croissants for her to wake up to. Strolling down the Champs-Élysées, dragging her through the Louvre to feed my insatiable Monet appetite. Riding up to Montmartre to buy our first oil painting together to commemorate the trip. Drinks at Bar Hemingway in the Ritz with a world-famous bartender.

Our last day we spent lounging on the lawn in front of the Eiffel Tower — beautiful sunny day, some bread, cheese, ham, and rosé. That was the one.

Venice

Italy

Venice was a trip. It felt like a movie set. We had a little boutique hotel tucked in a back, quiet alley — but we livened it up when we met Victor and Nicky and partied through the night, found some stray kittens, and had them sleepover on our floor. A blast in a really neat city.

Florence

Italy

Florence was more strolls, sandwiches, art, and pasta. We took the walk up to Piazzale Michelangelo and found a table at a restaurant at the top with views over the city. At some point the waitress came over, visibly concerned — they had a reservation in three hours. "Do you think you'll be finished at the table by then?" We were on a bottle of wine watching the city at sunset. Perfect.

Rome

Italy

In Rome you could feel the gravity of history. The Colosseum and its hordes of tourists. The Trevi Fountain tucked around some little side street corner — we had McDonald's vanilla cones and fries before we walked up to it. Seeing the Vatican and St. Peter's Basilica was truly incredible together.

Capri

Italy

We spent the day in Capri tooling around the island on our little dinghy — hopping in the water in coves, checking out the Blue Grotto, and spontaneously running into Kendall, Jordan, and their crew on our boat trip. We grabbed drinks and food with them to close out the day.

It was an incredible first trip together with so many memories to build our life upon. We love to travel together and this set the tone — it was possible, and we weren't going to kill each other traveling together. Which is a very positive sign.
